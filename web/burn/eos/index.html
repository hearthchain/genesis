The EOS burn account

eosio.null

copied

Works as a plain transfer from Anchor, Wombat, TokenPocket, OKX Wallet, or the Unicove web wallet. No memo is needed for the burn itself.

  • –Any amount, in one or several transactions, any time before the window closes.
  • –Burn and bind in any order; the credit follows the sending account.
  • –No Hearth address yet? Burn now, bind later: unbound burns wait in the journal and credit as soon as your binding arrives.

eosio.null is the chain's built-in null account: its permissions are provably unsatisfiable, so what arrives there can never move.

Never burn straight from an exchange. Withdraw to your own account first, then burn. Exchange accounts are blacklisted, exchange custody breaks the continuous holding period your credit is priced from, and a withdrawal sent directly to a burn account credits nobody.

Bind

Bind the account with a transfer memo.

EOS wallets cannot sign a bare statement, so the binding rides a memo instead: generate it below, then either put it in the burn transfer itself, or send a separate 0.0001 A transfer to eosio.null with it later, from the account you burn from. The transfer is signed by your account's key on chain, and that signature is the whole proof.

Your binding memo, copy it exactly

  1. Copy the memo above.
  2. Send your burn to eosio.null with this memo, or, if you burned already, send exactly 0.0001 A there with it.
  3. The binding shows in your cabinet once the transfer is confirmed on two independent sources.

The statement is hearth-genesis-binding:v1:<account>:<hearth>; only the latest binding per account counts at snapshot freeze, so a binding can be corrected by sending another memo.

Hearth genesis

Credit converts to HRTH one to one by the published formula. Loyalty to a fallen chain converts into a share of the new network. The starting supply is floating: the sum of all credited burns plus the published team and treasury share, so turnout sets it. Credit is not compensation, promises no dollar value, and implies no price floor for HRTH.
