Disputes

Every figure can be argued with. Here is how.

The backend is a cache, never an authority: every credit is recomputable from the published artifacts. If your recomputation disagrees with ours, the artifacts are what we argue over, and the dispute window exists exactly for that.

When a burn says mismatch

Every burn is cross-checked against two independent public nodes. mismatch means the nodes disagreed about the transaction; the burn is parked for manual review and is not credited until the disagreement is resolved. No action is needed from you unless it stays mismatched for more than a day.

When an address is blocked

The MVP prices histories made of plain transfers. An address whose history contains anything else (leases, DEX trades, invokes) is blocked to manual review instead of risking a wrong credit. Blocked addresses are listed in the snapshot's blockedSources; each is resolved by hand before the freeze.

Contesting a credit

Open an issue on the repository with the transaction ids, the addresses involved, and what you believe the correct figure is, ideally with the output of the published reproduction script. Disputes are accepted until the snapshot freezes; the frozen Merkle root is final.

Proving an address is yours

Control is proven the same way bindings work: by signing a challenge statement with the address's own key, in your wallet or with the bindsign CLI. We never ask for seed phrases; anyone who does is not us.

Burned from an exchange by mistake? A withdrawal sent straight from an exchange to the burn address credits the exchange's blacklisted address, not you, and signing a challenge cannot fix that: the exchange owns the key. This is the one case the dispute window cannot help with; it is why every page says to withdraw first.

Hearth genesis

Credit converts to HRTH one to one by the published formula. Loyalty to a fallen chain converts into a share of the new network. The starting supply is floating: the sum of all credited burns plus the published team and treasury share, so turnout sets it. Credit is not compensation, promises no dollar value, and implies no price floor for HRTH.
