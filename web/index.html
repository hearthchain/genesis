How it works

Four steps, none of them an account.

No registration, no custody, no personal data. The site never touches keys and never accepts funds; every signature happens in your own wallet.

01 · Get a Hearth address

Generate a Hearth address locally with the open-source console tooling in the repository. It is a new format: it cannot be derived from your old keys, and its seed never leaves your machine. In-browser generation ships before the window opens.

02 · Bind your source address

In the cabinet, sign one short statement with the wallet of the address you will burn from. That binds every burn from that source to your Hearth address. We never ask for a seed phrase; signing happens only inside your wallet extension.

03 · Burn

Send a plain transfer to the published burn address of the chain: no memo, no payload, any amount, any time before the window closes. The burn address is provably unspendable; the coins are gone for good.

04 · Watch it confirm

Watchers on two independent public nodes pick the burn up within minutes. It shows in your cabinet immediately, and is credited once it reaches confirmation depth and both nodes agree.

Crediting

Priced at the best week you held through.

The formula

Credit = amount burned × the coin's highest weekly average price over the period you continuously held it, from a published CoinMarketCap-based price log. Your history is split into layers by minimum balance: each top-up starts a new layer with its own date, each sale shaves layers down. Held through the whole fall: credited near the top. Bought recently: credited at recent prices. 1 credit = 1 HRTH.

1,000 WAVES held since March 2022 → peak week $49.71 → 49,710 HRTH
the same 1,000 WAVES bought in early 2024 → $4.00 → 4,000 HRTH

Until the snapshot freezes, the figure is a minimum: the best-week price can only grow. The published rule is integer arithmetic with truncation, so anyone can recompute every credit to the last digit.

Trust the files, not the site: the burn log, binding registry, price journal, and snapshot with its Merkle root are published as append-only artifacts with a reproduction script, from the pipeline repository.

Hearth genesis

Credit converts to HRTH one to one by the published formula. Loyalty to a fallen chain converts into a share of the new network. The starting supply is floating: the sum of all credited burns plus the published team and treasury share, so turnout sets it. Credit is not compensation, promises no dollar value, and implies no price floor for HRTH.
